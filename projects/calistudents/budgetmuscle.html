Building muscle on a budget: 4 tips to build muscle for poor students

Training and building your muscles can generally be a costly process: fitness membership, a healthy diet rich in protein, supplements, and potentially equipment.

If you're on a tight budget it can be hard to piece the puzzle together - That's why I've gathered these four tips to help you build muscle on a budget

1. You only need one supplement - Creatine

Despite what you might read - online you only need a single supplement if you eat a well-balanced, protein-rich diet.
That supplement is Creatine Monohydrate, most often just called creatine. There are multiple reasons it's the only supplement you need:

  • You don't get the optimal amount of creatine through your diet:
    Most supplements - e.g. protein powder, fish oils, BCAAs, etc. - are unnecessary if you eat a healthy, well-balanced, and protein-rich diet.
    You normally don't get the optimal amount of creatine through your diet - however healthy your diet may be.
    It should be said though that if you don't have a good diet - supplements are probably in your best interest.

  • Immediate improvement in your muscles by 5% on average:
    In short - creatine allows you to perform more repetitions of your exercises - which in turn allows you to squeeze more out of each workout.
    Long term muscle improvement has been shown to be as high as between 25-33%.
    Combine that with the fact that creatine stores itself in your muscles and draws water to them and you have a recipe for aesthetics as well as strength gain.

  • Creatine is cheap as hell:
    You only need 3-5g of creatine a day - and you can get 400-500g of creatine monohydrate powder for 100-150 DKK - many times even less if you keep an eye out for good deals.

2. Buy frozen goods

When it comes to vegetables you get the exact same - if not more - nutrition from frozen vegetables as you do from fresh vegetables.

Frozen vegetables are usually much cheaper than fresh vegetables. And they last much longer. This way you save many AND you prevent food waste.

3. Prep your meals

Mealprepping (preparing your meals for multiple days in advance) saves you not only money - but also time.

When you purchase in bulk for multiple meals you typically save money. There's plenty of content on the internet about mealprepping tips.

Low budget recipes

4. Eat cheap protein sources

You don't have to live off expensive meat to cover your protein needs for optimal protein synthesis. For example: The price of green lentils is much cheaper per gram of protein than - say - cashews.

You save a lot of money by going for the cheaper protein sources.
Remember - you only need roughly 1.4-2g of protein per kg bodyweight.
